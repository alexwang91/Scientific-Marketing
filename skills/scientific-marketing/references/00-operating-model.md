# Operating Model

## Core Position

Scientific marketing turns market uncertainty into decisions the team can test, fund, pause, or scale.

The sequence is: business decision, consumer mechanism, marketing lever, AI role, causal measurement, next decision.

AI can expand sensing, simulation, generation, and monitoring. It does not turn weak evidence into truth.

## Use When

Use this reference when the request is broad, vague, or strategic. It sets the suite's default stance.

## Method

1. Translate the request into a business decision.
2. Name the consumer behavior that must change.
3. Choose the marketing lever: product, price, channel, message, audience, timing, offer, or experience.
4. Decide the AI role: radar, miner, simulator, creator, operator, or scientist.
5. Define the measurement path before recommending scale.
6. State the next smallest useful experiment.

## Inputs

- Product, category, market, audience, and business goal.
- Current channel mix and funnel data if available.
- Constraints: budget, market, legal, brand, timeline.

## Outputs

- Scientific Marketing Brief.
- Hypothesis list.
- Measurement plan.
- Execution plan with owners and metrics.

## Validation

A good answer names the decision the team will make after the work.

A weak answer sounds strategic but leaves the team unable to choose, test, fund, pause, or scale anything.

## Failure Modes

- Treating correlation as causation.
- Calling AI output an insight without user evidence.
- Recommending channels before naming the buying mechanism.
- Using terms like ecosystem or full-funnel without an owner, metric, or action.

## Language Notes

Prefer concrete verbs: choose, test, compare, hold out, cut, fund, rewrite, launch, pause.

Avoid: empower, unlock, transform, deep integration, full-chain closed loop.
