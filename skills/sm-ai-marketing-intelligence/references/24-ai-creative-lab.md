# 24 AI Creative Lab

## Core Position

AI creative work should produce testable variation, not more content.

## Use When

Use this for hooks, claims, proof, scripts, product pages, ads, landing pages, lifecycle messages, and creator briefs.

## Method

1. Define the audience, job, barrier, and proof.
2. Choose the variable to test: hook, claim, proof, visual angle, CTA, offer, or channel.
3. Generate variants with one clear difference.
4. Map each variant to a hypothesis.
5. Define the metric and guardrail.

## Outputs

- Creative brief.
- Hook matrix.
- Claim and proof table.
- Channel-specific variants.
- Test matrix.

## Validation

Do not judge creative only by taste. Compare against attention, comprehension, click, conversion, brand recall, or downstream quality.

## Failure Modes

- Producing many variants with no hypothesis.
- Changing too many variables at once.
- Writing claims the product cannot support.

## Language Notes

Use "claim" only when there is proof. Use "angle" when the idea changes framing but not the product promise.
