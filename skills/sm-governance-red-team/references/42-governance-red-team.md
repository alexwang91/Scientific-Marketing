# 42 Governance Red Team

## Core Position

The more personalized and automated the marketing system becomes, the more explicit the risk review must be.

## Contains

Privacy, consent, bias, dark patterns, false claims, sensitive groups, AI-generated misinformation, brand safety, and escalation rules.

## Use When

Use this for personalization, nudges, AI agents, claims, targeting, health, finance, children, vulnerable groups, and regulated categories.

## Method

1. Identify data used.
2. Identify the audience and sensitivity.
3. Identify the claim and proof.
4. Identify user control and consent.
5. Identify possible harm.
6. Recommend safer design or block the tactic.

## Outputs

- Risk register.
- Required changes.
- Approval checklist.
- Blockers.

## Validation

A recommendation passes only when a reviewer can see the data source, user choice, proof, and harm mitigation.

## Failure Modes

- Treating consent as a checkbox.
- Hiding manipulative offers behind personalization.
- Making AI product claims without proof.
- Targeting anxiety or vulnerability.

## Language Notes

Use "risk" and "approval required." Avoid vague reassurance.
