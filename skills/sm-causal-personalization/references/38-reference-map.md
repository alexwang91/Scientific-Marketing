## Core Position

Do not re-search the basic theory every time the skill runs.

## Theory Anchors

### Personalization As Causal Inference

Use for the overall frame: personalization should be learned through experiments, heterogeneous treatment effects, policy evaluation, and policy learning.

Reference:

- Advancing Personalization: How to Experiment, Learn & Optimize, Columbia/Duke/HBS working paper.

Use when explaining:

- Why personalization is a causal problem.
- Why test-and-learn matters.
- Why policy learning is more useful than static segment rules.
- Why foundation models can support personalization but do not remove the need for experiments.

## Search Rules

Search only when:

- The user asks for latest papers, benchmarks, tools, or vendor capabilities.
- A public claim needs precise citation.
- Platform rules, privacy rules, or ad-channel policies may have changed.
- The work maps causal segments to ad platform audiences, retail media controls, ecommerce bidding controls, or creator/KOL marketplaces.
- The output names specific creators, media, countries, price ranges, available audience controls, creator marketplace availability, country audience size, reachable share, or expected ROI.
- The output depends on current CPM, CPC, CPA, creator fee, product category availability, platform planner estimate, or API capability.
- The module is being updated with a new research branch.

Do not search when:

- The question is about core definitions already covered here.
- The output is an internal working brief.
- The user needs a first causal personalization design.

## Source Quality Order

Prefer:

1. Peer-reviewed papers or working papers from credible institutions.
2. Official technical docs from research groups, labs, or tool maintainers.
3. Industrial research blogs with clear methodology.
4. Vendor blogs only for implementation examples, not theory.

Avoid:

- Blog posts that equate personalization with propensity targeting.
- Claims that synthetic consumers replace holdouts.
- Case studies without baseline, control, or cost accounting.
