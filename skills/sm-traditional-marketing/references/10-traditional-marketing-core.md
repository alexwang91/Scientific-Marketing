# 10 Traditional Marketing Core

## Core Position

AI does not replace STP, positioning, pricing, channel, or CRM. It helps you do those jobs with more evidence and less manual work.

## Contains

STP, 4P, 5C, AARRR, funnel, brand equity, GTM, CRM, pricing, channel, CAC/LTV, RFM, lifecycle, and sales-marketing handoff.

## Use When

Use this when the request asks for strategy, campaign plan, GTM, funnel, growth, CRM, positioning, channel, or pricing.

## Method

1. Define the target segment and buying situation.
2. Name the job, pain, trigger, and substitute.
3. Define the offer: product, proof, price, packaging, channel.
4. Map the funnel or lifecycle stage.
5. Pick the marketing lever that can change behavior.
6. Define the metric and the test.

## Execution

Ask for missing basics only when they block the decision. Otherwise make explicit assumptions and label them.

## Outputs

- Strategy diagnosis.
- Segment and buying situation.
- Lever recommendation.
- Funnel or lifecycle map.
- Testable next action.

## Validation

The strategy must answer: who buys, why now, why us, through which path, at what cost, and what evidence would change our mind.

## Failure Modes

- Jumping to AI tactics before naming the segment.
- Treating awareness, conversion, and retention as one problem.
- Saying "brand building" without memory structure or category entry points.

## Language Notes

Keep terms like STP, 4P, GTM, CRM, CAC/LTV, RFM, and funnel. Avoid vague phrases like full-chain empowerment or growth flywheel unless translated into actions.
