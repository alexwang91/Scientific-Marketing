# 11 Market Category Diagnosis

## Core Position

Category work explains the battlefield. It should expose demand growth, substitutes, entry barriers, user tasks, channel power, and language shifts.

## Contains

Market size, category maturity, growth drivers, substitutes, competitor map, category entry points, adoption barriers, user tasks, price bands, and channel structure.

## Use When

Use this for market entry, new product launch, competitor review, category strategy, or annual planning.

## Method

1. Define the category boundary.
2. Map demand drivers and constraints.
3. Identify substitutes, not just direct competitors.
4. Compare competitor promises, proof, price, channel, and audience.
5. Identify category entry points and user tasks.
6. State the market opportunity as a testable bet.

## How To Find

Use embedded knowledge by default. Search only for current market data, pricing, product specs, competitor moves, or platform behavior.

Useful sources when current facts matter: industry reports, company filings, earnings calls, app store reviews, Amazon or BestBuy reviews, TikTok and YouTube comments, competitor sites, retailer pages, search trend tools, and sales field notes.

## Outputs

- Category map.
- Competitor promise and proof table.
- Demand signal board.
- Opportunity hypotheses.

## Validation

Good category diagnosis changes a decision: product claim, target segment, channel priority, price, or launch sequence.

## Failure Modes

- Confusing category size with accessible demand.
- Listing competitors without comparing the job they solve.
- Treating competitor language as user language.

## Language Notes

Use "substitute" when users solve the same job another way. Use "category entry point" when describing situations that trigger memory and purchase.
