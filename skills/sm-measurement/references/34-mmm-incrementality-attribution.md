# 34 MMM Incrementality Attribution

## Core Position

Attribution assigns credit. Incrementality estimates value. MMM can support budget decisions when user-level tracking is incomplete or channels interact.

## Contains

Marketing mix modeling, adstock, saturation, response curve, baseline, seasonality, budget scenarios, attribution limits, and experiment calibration.

## Use When

Use this for budget allocation, channel planning, long-running media, privacy-constrained measurement, offline channels, and multi-channel effects.

## Method

1. Define KPI and business unit.
2. Gather spend, impressions, price, promotions, seasonality, distribution, and external controls.
3. Model baseline and media response.
4. Estimate adstock and saturation.
5. Calibrate with experiments where possible.
6. Generate budget scenarios with uncertainty.

## Outputs

- MMM readiness checklist.
- Channel response curves.
- Budget scenarios.
- Uncertainty notes.

## Validation

MMM needs stable data, sensible priors or constraints, and calibration. It should not overrule clean experiments without explanation.

## Failure Modes

- Using MMM as a black box.
- Ignoring promotions, price, distribution, or seasonality.
- Reporting exact ROI without uncertainty.
- Treating attribution and MMM as the same thing.

## Language Notes

Use "scenario" when results depend on assumptions.
